Queer Themes in Bengali Literature: From Silence to Self-Expression

By Ankan Kumar Paul

Throughout time, literature has always been a reflection of society and has represented society’s values, fears, prejudices and hopes – so has been the case for the literature of the world at large and, in this case, for in that history, a few voiceless and yet essential voices for the world of literature have not been acknowledged. One example of this is the absence of queer individuals from the literary landscape; queer voices have historically been silenced, repressed, or misrepresented in literature, whether in terms of their experiences, their desires (sexual) or their identity. Bengali literature, which has been traditionally recognised for being rich in humanism and progressive in its traditions, has taken a major step forward in terms of representing queer lives in recent years. The development from the absence of literature, to literature that gives voice to the voices of the voiceless, helps to illuminate not only the development of literature over time, but also the changing social attitudes regarding gender, sexuality and society itself.

The development of the representation of queer individuals in Bengali literature is inextricably linked with the overall evolution of social conservatism throughout South Asia; traditional societies within this region, as well as in several others, were traditionally dominated by rigid understandings of gender and sexuality and therefore, queer identities could not exist in those places. Because of this, it is not really surprising to see that queer experiences and/or identities would be buried underneath layers of metaphor, ambiguity and silence in the writing of the time. Additionally, the few instances where there was even reference to the existence of same-sex attraction and/or gender non-conformity in literature, they were not publicly identified; writers used indirect language and coded language to express what could not be expressed directly.

You can find evidence for early written expressions of queer awareness in Bengali literature, and it is not always overtly queer; simply put, the emotional turmoil of two friends or their willing acceptance of fluidity as it is expressed in mythological revisions has provided a means for creating spaces in which to imagine alternative identity through the depiction of more than one alternative social role. In most cases, alternative identity was not easily articulated, and even so, the way that alternative identity was expressed was often indirect and lacked an adequate vocabulary to describe such alternative experiences.

The work of Rabindranath Tagore, while never overtly queer, was almost always subverting the established constructs of gender roles. Characters such as Chitrangada, Labanya and others in Tagore’s writing show a depth and complexity of emotional response which is more than simple adherence to the social constructs of femininity. By opening up the exploration of one’s self and being an individual, Tagore opened up the possibility for questioning the categories of social identity that one is placed in. Similarly, many other Bengali writers examined issues of identity and desire as well as isolation and alienation, which can be interpreted using today’s sociological lens looking at issues of queer identity.

The world saw a shift between late-20th and early-21st centuries. Global conversations about LGBTQ+ rights/feminist movements, and human rights discourses, began having an impact on the literature produced from Bengal. Writers started transitioning from only using coded representations to an increasingly direct addressing of queer identities in their writings. Literature became a space where those who are otherwise marginalized can tell their own stories instead of have their stories told by others.

Contemporary Bengali queer literature is richly diverse in voice. Contemporary writers write about many different topics like love between individuals of the same sex, transgender identity, exclusion from society, rejection from one’s family, mental health issues, and searching for belongingness. These narratives challenge the notion that heterosexuality is the only valid form of desire. More importantly, they provide a human face to queer individuals by portraying them as human beings with hope, dreams, fear, relationships, and aspirations.

Queer autobiographical writing is a major development; personal narratives provide a unique perspective on the difficulties that LGBTQ individuals face in a world that does not acknowledge their existence. Memoirs, essay, and life writing allow queer authors to reclaim the power of defining themselves. In doing so, these works push back against stereotypes and reveal some of the psychological impact of oppression and being rendered invisible in society.

In addition, there has also been a significant increase in the representation of transgender experiences within Bengali literature. Historically, transgender people have faced significant marginalization despite their existence throughout the history of South Asia. The newer body of work in this literature more frequently acknowledges the resilience, cultural contributions, and daily issues faced by transgender people. Such works encourage readers to re-think preconceived gender roles and what it means to be human

Bengali little magazines, independent publishing, and digital platforms have had a significant impact on exploring queer themes. These types of platforms allow new writers to showcase their writing regardless of whether it adheres to the traditional standards of commercial publishing. The use of alternative forms of media has made it possible for Queer writers to have their work distributed more widely and assist in building support networks or communities.

There have also been many changes in both the legal and social spheres in India that have created a shift in the way literary texts are being created. The momentous 2018 ruling in Navtej Singh Johar v. Union of India which decriminalised homosexuality was a paradigm shift for the overall landscape of the rights of LGBTQ+ individuals. Even though legal recognition does not mean that prejudice no longer exists, it has encouraged more candid conversations about sexuality and identity. Through literature, empathy and understanding continue to be cultivated between readers and authors in this new-found political climate.

In many cases, queer characters still have a tendency to be represented as mere caricatures, or only seen through the lens of trauma and suffering. For authentic representation to occur, literature must move past tokenized representations and recognize the true breadth of queerness that exists within queer communities. The exploration of queer life represented in literature needs to continue to depict the full diversity of queerness, including creativity, love, joy, friendship and everyday living. The matter of queer themes in Bengali literature goes beyond mere representation; they also together challenge and transform the established structures through which society operates, giving rise to a future that is more connected and open to inclusivity in the viewer's mind and by creating the capacity for hope in the lives of many people experiencing similar feelings. They demonstrate that literature is not only a representation of the society created within, but rather a tool that can fundamentally alter the course of society's development. By generating awareness of the existence of those who have experienced exclusion due to their sexual orientation, literature is expounding Chris Bellos' definition of what it is to be human. The exchange between literature and a person's ability to express themselves freely in Bengali literature is therefore an expression of courage, resistance to oppression and hope for the future. It embodies the relentless pursuit of queer individuals to find visibility and self-worth within a clearly separate framework in writing and social existence. As more forms of Bengali literature develop along the same trajectory as queer literature currently has, its focus on queer thematic elements will continue to add dimension to its inherently humanistic literature and will affirm the continued necessity for diverse perspectives on life in the future. The celebration of these voices in literature provides not only a medium for recounting new stories, but also presents an opportunity to create a more generous and inclusive society.
